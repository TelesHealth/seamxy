👗 New Feature: “Custom Fit Marketplace”

Feature Name: Custom Work Requests / Bespoke Orders

🎯 Goal

Allow users to:

Submit custom design or tailoring requests (e.g., suits, gowns, dresses).

Receive quotes or proposals from local or online custom clothiers, tailors, and designers.

Compare prices, timelines, and portfolios — and even chat before ordering.

🧩 User Flow

User Inputs Measurements & Style Preferences
– From their profile or previous saved data.
– Option to upload reference photos or design ideas.

Select “Request Custom Design”
– Choose category (Suit, Dress, Gown, Traditional Attire, etc.)
– Describe details (fabric type, event, color, delivery deadline).

AI Prepares Auto-Matched Suggestions
– Suggests potential tailors or boutiques that match the style and budget.
– Generates a “Request for Quote” (RFQ) template.

Tailors / Designers Respond
– Registered B2B partners can send back quotes, timelines, and sample designs.
– Option to upload sketches or show previous work.

User Reviews & Accepts
– Can filter by price, quality rating, delivery time, or location.
– In-app chat or video consultation option.

Transaction Handling (Phase 2)
– Enable secure payments or deposits once you have a payment partner (Stripe, PayPal, etc.).

💼 Admin Panel Additions

For your admin view:

Manage B2B tailor/designer accounts.

Review and approve RFQs and proposals.

Monitor pricing, commissions, and fulfillment feedback.

Add premium listing options for verified tailors.

💰 Monetization Opportunities

Commission per order (e.g., 5–10% of each completed custom order).

Subscription tiers for designers (basic/free vs. premium for higher visibility).

Promoted placement in “Featured Tailors” or “Verified Designers.”

Design request credits for users (limit free requests, then paid).

🧠 AI + Personalization

Your AI stylist could:

Translate vague inputs (“I want something classy for a summer wedding”) into detailed design briefs.

Recommend tailors who match that aesthetic.

Predict estimated cost ranges based on materials and complexity.
