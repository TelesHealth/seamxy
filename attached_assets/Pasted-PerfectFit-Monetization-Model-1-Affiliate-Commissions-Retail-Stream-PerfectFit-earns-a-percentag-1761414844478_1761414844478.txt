PerfectFit Monetization Model
1. Affiliate Commissions (Retail Stream)

PerfectFit earns a percentage from every “Quick Buy” sale completed through affiliate links (Amazon, ASOS, Shopify, etc.).

Each product in recommendations includes a unique affiliate tracking URL.

Typical affiliate rates range from 4–10% per transaction.

Example:

A user buys a $120 pair of chinos from Amazon → PerfectFit earns ~$6.

2. Bespoke Commissions (Custom Orders)

When a user selects a custom maker and places a deposit, PerfectFit charges a platform fee.

Recommended structure:

10% commission on every successful custom order (deposit and/or balance).

Optionally, the platform can collect payments via Stripe Connect to automatically split the funds between tailor and PerfectFit.

3. Maker Subscriptions (Recurring Revenue)

Tailors and custom clothiers can upgrade their visibility via subscription plans:

Basic ($0/month) — listed and can receive RFQs (requests for quote).

Pro ($29/month) — higher ranking, access to user insights, and early RFQ notifications.

Elite ($99/month) — featured placement in search, promoted brand page, and verified badge.

4. Sponsored Listings

Retailers and makers can pay for featured placement in search results or “Editor’s Choice” slots.

Sponsored posts can appear in:

“Recommended for You” feed

“Featured Makers” carousel

“Seasonal Trends” showcase

This creates non-intrusive ad revenue without cluttering user experience.

5. Premium User Tier (Optional Future Phase)

Users can upgrade to a Premium subscription for added AI styling features:

Personalized “Capsule Wardrobe” generation.

Monthly outfit recommendations.

Exclusive discounts from partner brands.

Suggested pricing: $5.99/month or $49/year.

6. Data Insights & Analytics (B2B)

PerfectFit’s anonymized data can provide insights to:

Fashion brands (trend detection)

Fabric suppliers (demand forecasting)

Tailoring franchises (market demand by region)

Offer as subscription analytics dashboards or reports.

7. Transaction Fees (Operational Revenue)

For every in-app Stripe transaction (retail or bespoke):

Maker absorbs Stripe processing fee (typically 2.9% + 30¢)

PerfectFit takes commission net of Stripe fee.

8. Future Expansion: White-Label SaaS

Offer PerfectFit’s engine as a white-label platform for:

Independent tailors or boutiques.

Corporate uniform programs.

Custom fashion startups.

Licensing model: Setup fee + monthly SaaS fee.
